The negative-split rule

If you cross the half marathon mat in 2:02 or faster, you went out too hot. Slow to 9:35 for two miles. The wall is real and it kills people who bank time. Even 4:08 finishers run a slight positive split (mile 14–22 is 5–10 sec/mi slower than first half). That's fine. What is not fine is a 4-min positive split because you blew up at mile 22.

Race-day timeline

4:30 AM
Wake
Lights on, water immediately. No snooze. Body needs the head start.
4:45 AM
Coffee + race breakfast
Bagel with peanut butter and honey · banana · coffee · 16 oz water with electrolytes. ~600 cal.
5:30 AM
Bathroom · final kit check
Gear list below. Body Glide everything. Pin bib. Phone charged.
5:45 AM
Leave for Pentagon
Metro to Pentagon stop, or Uber. Allow 60 min minimum — security and crowd. Bag check opens 5:30.
6:30 AM
Arrive Pentagon · porta-potty line
Last bathroom is non-negotiable. Lines are 30+ minutes by 7 AM. Sip water until 7:15.
7:30 AM
Bag check · enter corral
Throwaway long sleeve until corral. Light jog, dynamic stretches.
7:40 AM
First gel + sip
15 min before gun. Don't take a caffeine gel — save those for mile 16 and 20.
7:55 AM
HOWITZER · Gun fires
Don't sprint the start. Crowded for 1.5 miles. First mile slower than goal pace. This is a 26-mile race, not a 100m sprint.
11:55 AM – 12:15 PM
Iwo Jima finish
Marine puts the medal over your head. Walk a few minutes. Eat anything offered. Find dry clothes within 30 minutes — heat loss is fast.

Practiced fuel only

Every gel in this plan is something you've taken on long runs. Race day is not the day to try a new flavor, brand, or caffeine level. GI distress at mile 18 ends a marathon faster than the wall does.

Mental scripts for the dark miles

Mile 18–22: when the body asks if you can do this

Every marathoner has a scripted answer ready. You wrote yours during the long runs. Choose three. Use them in rotation. Say them out loud if you have to.

"This is exactly what I trained for. The 18-miler with MP. I did this."
"Just to the next aid station. That's it. Don't think past the next aid station."
"My legs are heavy. So is everyone else's. Keep moving."
"This pain is information. It's not damage. I trained for this."
"Form check: shoulders down, arms relaxed, breathe deep, foot under hip."
"30,000 people are out here suffering with me. We chose this. I chose this."

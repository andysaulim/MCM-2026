Critical: cut ends Aug 17

You cannot cut calories during peak training. Body needs full fuel for 18-mile runs and threshold work. If you haven't lost the weight by Aug 17, accept the weight you're at and switch to maintenance. Holding the line is more important than dropping more pounds at the cost of a successful race.

Supplements — what's worth taking, what's not

Vegetarian endurance athletes have specific shortfalls. Address these directly. Most other supplements are noise.

SupplementDaily doseWhy
B12500 mcgVegetarians are at risk of deficiency. Critical for red blood cells (oxygen transport for endurance).
Iron18 mg (with vit C)Endurance training depletes iron. Vegetarian non-heme iron is poorly absorbed. Take with citrus or vitamin C, not with coffee or tea.
Vitamin D32,000 IUBone health, immune function. Deficiency common in office workers regardless of diet.
Magnesium glycinate300–400 mg before bedSleep quality, muscle relaxation. Glycinate form is best absorbed and doesn't cause GI distress like other forms.
Creatine monohydrate5gSurprisingly useful for endurance — strength maintenance during high-volume training. Vegetarians benefit most because diet provides ~zero creatine.
Whey/pea protein1–2 scoopsHits 140g protein target. Not technically a supplement; it's just convenient food.
Omega-3 (algae or fish oil)1–2g EPA/DHAAnti-inflammatory. Vegetarians who don't eat fish should use algae-based.
Skip these

Pre-workouts, BCAAs, glutamine, ZMA, "fat burners," beta-alanine pills, testosterone boosters, "endurance blends." None have evidence justifying the cost. The marketing is louder than the science.
